The Mission

We have received an urgent mission! Our esteemed President, TD, has been "kidnapped," and we need your unique skills, creativity, and expertise to rescue him. We're holding a Hackathon event that will not only put your problem-solving abilities to the test but also serve as an opportunity for team bonding and exploration.

Goals Of The Hackathon

  • Spark enthusiasm for using AI tools like ChatGPT and GitHub CoPilot.

  • Foster a deeper understanding of how these tools can impact the way we work together.

  • Develop expertise to help us deliver more value to our clients.

Briefing

  1. Assemble Your Elite Team

    Each branch must form a special task force of cross-discipline members, including design, development, testing, and project management experts.

  2. Choose your rescue mission app

    Each team will brainstorm and choose an app that could play a critical role in rescuing TD. Make sure to think of ways to incorporate ChatGPT, GitHub CoPilot, and various APIs to streamline the development process and enhance the app's functionality.

  3. Scope out the operation

    Determine the scope and requirements for an app that can be feasibly built within a 3-hour time frame.

  4. Harness the power of AI

    Maximize the use of ChatGPT and GitHub CoPilot throughout the development process, including requirements documentation, code generation, design ideas, problem-solving, and more.

  5. Build your prototype

    • Create a user-friendly UI for managing suspects, evidence, and other critical information related to the rescue mission.
    • Develop backend logic for processing input and interacting with ChatGPT and APIs.
    • Integrate with APIs for gathering valuable information and unveiling unexpected twists in the investigation.

Debriefing

  1. Showcase your creation

    Each team will present their app to the other branches, demonstrating its functionality and how it can assist in solving the mystery.

  2. Share your learnings

    Discuss the insights gained during the Hackathon, including new techniques, AI tool usage, and potential applications for future projects.

  3. Celebrate your success

    Take a moment to appreciate the hard work and creativity that went into building your app, and enjoy the camaraderie with your colleagues.

Rules

  1. team formation

    Participants will sign up individually, and based on the volume of interest, we will form one or more balanced teams per office location. Each team will include a mix of role. This approach encourages cross-discipline collaboration and helps ensure that each team has the necessary skills to complete their app.

  2. pre-event preparations

    Team members are allowed to arrange licenses and learn technologies in advance, but they must not write any code for their team's app prior to the event's start time. This ensures a level playing field for all participants.

  3. cross-discipline collaboration

    There are no strict boundaries for team members to stick to their primary discipline during the event. We encourage participants to be flexible and contribute to various aspects of the project as needed, fostering creativity and collaboration within the team.

  4. time

    Each team will have exactly 180 minutes to plan, design, and develop their app. This includes design, coding, testing, and finalizing the prototype. Teams must ensure they complete their work within the allotted time.

  5. code submission

    Upon completion of the development phase, teams must commit their final code to a project in WillowTree's GitHub account. This will enable the organization to view the work and learn from the teams' experiences.

  6. post-development activities

    After the development phase, team members should take some time to synthesize their learnings and prepare a presentation to share their experiences, insights, and the app's functionality with the organization.

  7. AI tool usage

    To demonstrate the potential of AI tools, we encourage participants to use AI technologies like ChatGPT and GitHub CoPilot as much as possible throughout the event. Be creative in finding ways to integrate these tools into your development process and maximize their impact on your project.

FAQs

  • Can I use open-source resources in the app we develop during the hackathon?

    You're welcome to use any open source tooling or libraries that exist, but your application's own source code should all be written “from scratch” during the hackathon.

  • What resources will be provided to us during the Hackathon? Can we request additional tools or equipment?

    NEED TO UPDATE, but we encourage the use of ChatGPT, Bard, GitHub CoPilot, and any other approved AI tools.

  • What are the judging criteria for the hackathon?

    Our main goal is to foster enthusiasm, experiment, and learn. There won't be set criteria with 1st, 2nd, 3rd place, etc., but there will be awards in different categories.

  • Can I work on my own project, or do I need to form a team?

    One of our goals for this current hackathon is to understand how AI tooling affects the processes of teams collaborating together, so for this one, registered participants will be put into teams based on office and/or time zone. However, that does not mean you can't experiment with things on your own! If you do something fun that you want to share, we have a page for that in our Confluence space too. There's also the potential for future hackathons of a different format, goal, etc. pending the outcomes of this one.

  • Can I attend the hackathon remotely or do I need to be physically present?

    WFA Trees will be on teams with other WFA Trees, with an effort to group teams by time zone (pending volume of registered attendees). The hope is that WFN Trees will be able to find time during in-office days the week of the hackathon to participate. A DRI per-location will be responsible for coordinating schedules.
